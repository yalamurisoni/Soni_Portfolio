About

The short version: a quality engineer who likes systems, structure, and a good story.

I'm an SDET at Capgemini, where I spend my days making sure insurance business workflows on the Guidewire InsuranceSuite platform behave the way they're supposed to — and catching it when they don't. My toolkit centers on Gauge, Taiko, and Playwright in JavaScript, stitched into Jenkins pipelines with GitHub for version control, and I lean on GitHub Copilot to move faster without cutting corners.

Underneath the test scripts is a foundation in OOPS, data structures and algorithms, SDLC/STLC, and ETL — the kind of grounding that makes it easier to reason about why a system breaks, not just that it did. Lately I've been pulled toward API testing and Gen AI-assisted QA workflows, exploring how large language models can shrink the distance between writing a test and trusting it.

Before this, I built a facial-recognition attendance system from the ground up and got it published at IEEE ICOECA 2024 — proof that I like following a problem from idea to working software, not just the testing half of it.

Currently

When I'm not chasing flaky tests, I'm usually a few chapters into something — non-fiction on how systems and people work, the occasional novel for balance. Reading is where I slow down and think in longer sentences than a test assertion allows.
